## Wang Tang

PhD Student
School of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science
The University of Queensland, Australia

## Biography

I am a PhD student at The University of Queensland. My research focuses on AI infrastructure security, cloud-native systems, Kubernetes security, software security, and program analysis.

Before starting my PhD, I worked for more than ten years in cloud computing, distributed systems, Kubernetes-based platforms, AI infrastructure, and enterprise software engineering. My industrial experience has shaped my research interest in the security, reliability, and correctness of real-world AI and cloud-native systems.

My current research explores security problems in modern AI infrastructure, especially Kubernetes-based AI platforms, admission control, RBAC, GPU resource management, container orchestration, and LLM-driven vulnerability discovery.



##  Education

I am pursuing a PhD in AI for Security at The University of Queensland. Prior to this, I obtained an MSc in Advanced Computer Science from the University of Leeds and a BSc in Electronic Information Science and Technology from Nanjing University.

## Professional Experience

Before starting my PhD, I accumulated over ten years of industrial experience in cloud computing, distributed systems, Kubernetes, AI infrastructure, and enterprise software engineering. I worked as a Technical Manager and AI Architect at China Telecom, where I was involved in large-scale AI platforms, Kubernetes-based infrastructure, AI training and inference systems, and GPU resource management. Prior to that, I worked as an AI platform R&D engineer at Blueridge AI, focusing on cloud and edge AI platforms, MLOps infrastructure, and Kubernetes-based application orchestration. I also served as a technical lead at Shanghai Fushenlan Information Technology, where I led the development of enterprise microservice systems, insurance platforms, and data analysis platforms. Earlier in my career, I worked as a Java developer at Migu Culture Technology, contributing to backend systems, mobile Internet platforms, and distributed service architectures. These industry experiences have shaped my current research interests in the security, reliability, and correctness of real-world AI and cloud-native systems.
